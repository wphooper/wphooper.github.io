Translation Surfaces

A presentation of a translation surface a finite disjoint union of polygons in the plane with pairs of edges identified by translations which reverse the induced orientation of edges. Two presentations represent the same surface if they differ by a sequence of moves involving subdividing polygons, translating polygons, and gluing polygons along identified edges.

Vertices of polygons are identified to form cone angles with cone angle $2k\pi$ for some integer $k \geq 1$. A cone singularity with cone angle $2 \pi$ is removable. A translation surface determines a topological surface $S$ together with a finite collection $\Sigma \supset S$ of unremovable singularities.

Deforming translation surfaces

A translation surface can be deformed by deforming the polygons in a presentation while keeping identified pairs of edges parallel and of the same length.

We denote by ${\mathcal H}$ the collection of translation surfaces reachable by deformations of a fixed surface. The space ${\mathcal H}$ is an orbifold locally modeled on ${\mathbb C}^n$ for some $n \geq 1$ with transition functions in a symplectic group. The space ${\mathcal H}_1$ of unit area surfaces has finite volume due to work of Masur and Veech.

A rel deformation of a translation surface can be viewed as moving all vertices representing the same singularity simultaneously by the same translation of the plane. The rel leaf of a translation surface is the set of surfaces obtainable by a sequence of rel deformations.
